Mapping Malaysia's Foreign Arrivals

The global pandemic has drastically altered international travel, and Malaysia saw a massive decline in foreign arrivals.

Question is: How is Malaysia doing now? Scroll down to explore !

Which Point of Entry Has The Highest Traffic?

The map shows the key points of entry into Malaysia, highlighting where foreign arrivals are most concentrated. The largest symbols on the map represent the highest traffic. It is clear that the Johor has the has the highest traffic of foreign arrivals.

Bangunan Sultan Iskandar & Gelang Patah which are situated in Johor are leading as the top entry location. This shows that Johor experiences the highest traffic due to its strategic location at the Malaysia-Singapore border. Every day, thousands of Malaysians and Singaporeans cross between the two countries for work, leisure, and trade, making it one of the busiest land crossings in the world.

How Have Foreign Arrivals to Malaysia Changed Over the Years?

The area chart below shows the trends in foreign arrivals into Malaysia from 2020 to 2023, seperated by gender. It clearly shows the significant decline in arrivals at the start of 2020, coinciding with the global COVID-19 pandemic.

However, starting from early 2022, we see a gradual recovery as Malaysia reopens its borders and international travel begins to pick up again. The trend shows consistent growth, with both male and female arrivals increasing steadily. By late 2023, arrivals reach a stable point, reflecting the normalization of travel post-pandemic.

The pie chart shows the proportion of females and males arrivals into Malaysia. It shows that 57.2% of the total arrivals were male, while the remaining 42.8% were female.

This suggests a slight male dominance in foreign travel to Malaysia. which may be influenced by factors such as work-related travel and cross-border commuting, especially in regions like Johor where daily crossings are common. The gender proportions remain relatively stable throughout the period, as also reflected in the area chart tracking changes over time.

Which States Receive the Most Foreign Arrivals?

The bar chart above shows the number of foreign arrivals into Malaysia, broken down by state. Johor stands out with the highest number of arrivals, far surpassing other states. Meanwhile, Selangor, which includes Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA), ranks second in terms of foreign arrivals. As the main international gateway for air travelers into Malaysia, it is a major entry point for tourists, business travelers, and international connections.

In contrast, states like Terengganu, Pahang, and Melaka exhibit significantly lower arrival numbers due to their smaller international entry points, limited air traffic, and geographical factors. These states tend to attract more domestic than international tourists, resulting in fewer foreign arrivals compared to Johor and Selangor.

The disparity in arrival numbers highlights the impact of accessibility and regional connectivity on tourism patterns within Malaysia, illustrating how certain states benefit from their strategic locations while others face challenges in attracting international visitors.

"Which Countries Send the Most Visitors to Malaysia?"

The bar chart highlights the top countries contributing to foreign arrivals in Malaysia by showing the total arrivals from each nation over the recent years. It reveals that Singapore ranks as the highest contributor, followed by Indonesia and Thailand, demonstrating the dominance of Southeast Asian countries and the accessibility of travel between these nations.

This chart effectively illustrates the significant role that neighboring countries play in shaping Malaysia's tourism landscape, emphasizing the importance of regional connections in fostering international arrivals.
